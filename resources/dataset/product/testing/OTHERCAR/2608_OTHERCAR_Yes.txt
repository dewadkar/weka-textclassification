            Shopping for a 6 cylinder, 5 speed manual, 4 door, midsize sedan in North America is an exercise in frustration and lack of choice. Thank you Nissan for providing a product for those of us who still enjoy &quot;rowing our own&quot; transmission. North American makers have completely abandoned this class of driver. 

My 99 Maxima has been terrific so far. Absolute peach of an engine, good handling/ride balance, near-ideal size in a cost effective, efficient package.

Drab, forgettable styling, especially at the rump, lack of creative stowage space, a less than world-class manual shifter, rear-seat pass-through (not fold-down), a cold weather dash squeek, and disappointing bass response from the Bose system are all relatively minor niggles to date.

Nissan's styling trend for the Maxima is distressing. The early 90s version was a standout in its day and still looks good a decade later -- the mid 90s version is rounded and homogenized, looking like the &quot;Camraccords&quot; of its day -- and the new 2000 has the nose and tail treatment of a stylist &quot;forcing&quot; a response to complaints that the mid-90's version is boring and failing -- looking like a &quot;Nissan Impalamalibu&quot;...<br><br>

            I have had the unfortunate pleasure of owning a VW Golf GTI. The car was fire engine red and screamed at me from the dealership as I drove by. Two hours later I was back on the road with my shiney 'new' car. 
I was on the top of the world, this was my dream car. 5 speed, cruise, cd player and power locks. Oh My God! This was everything I wanted! 
This elation continued until one chilly morning I went to goto work and the car wouldn't start, leaving me trudging through the snow. Thank goodness for VW's 'Guarenteed Mobility', the car was towed on a flatbed truck 220 kms (130 miles) to the nearest dealership. It was discovered the timing belt had slipped cogs leaving the engine out of time, lucky I didn't bash in my valves with the piston. The second time the engine failed a fresh air intake ended up sticking open and sealed the intake manifold with ice. The third time again the timing skipped leaving my 8 month pregnant and myself stranded on the highway at two o'clock in the morning. 
The fan blower motor died in the dead cold of winter, and I had to drive the car those 220 kms to the dealership, with no heat, that was one chilly ride!!      
One may think all this bad VW luck would have occurred over some length of time, however, the car was purchased and sold within a 14 month period of time. 
All together, my experience with that car was similar to that of a...

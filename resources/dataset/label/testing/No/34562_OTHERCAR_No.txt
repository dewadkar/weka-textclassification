            Throughout 1998 I did a test drive of several SUVs , including the Infiniti QX4, before finally purchasing a 1999 Jeep Grand Cherokee Ltd. I was initially attracted to the QX4 because of the Infiniti name. I have always liked the looks of Infiniti sedans and the aura of quality that surrounds them.

Such was not the case for the QX4. To me not only was the QX4 obviously underpowered, it was also very over-priced. I liked the QX4's looks, but this is not a very good reason to buy an SUV. There was one major downside to the QX4's looks: a &quot;fake wood&quot; interior trim. Even the Jeep that I ended up buying had a fake wood trim, but it was one of the few negatives. Even the showroom salesman admitted that the two biggest complaints received about the QX4 were the fake wood and the lack of power for the price. Here is a simple equation that describes my QX4 experience: 

      fake wood + underpowered engine + overpriced = no sale.

I liked the looks of the Mitsubishi Montero Sport even more than the QX4, but again, it also lacked the engine power. If either the Montero Sport or Infiniti QX4 was offered with a V8 engine they would have garnered more market consideration and could get by with a list price above that of the Jeep Grand Cherokee Ltd of $34,000 due to the perception of higher reliability of Japanese designs - whether this is true or not.

I thought Infiniti would mean &quot;quality&quot;, but it turns out Infiniti means &quot;Nissan&quot;, and Nissan means &quot;a once great Japanese automotive powerhouse that has fallen on hard times and can't get up&quot;. Although, its trying.<br><br>

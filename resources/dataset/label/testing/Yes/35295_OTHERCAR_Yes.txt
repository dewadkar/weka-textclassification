            I worked for a car rental company for more than 4 years.  We ran Corsicas as our anchor car.  Why?  Simple...cost.  The cars on the auction market came at $7000.00 give or take.  They were 2 years old and had about 25000 to 30000 miles on them.  

Maintenance costs were minimal even as the cars racked up the miles.  Most of them had doubled their mileage in the first year and no major problems came up.  

This was a car that was simple to operate.  All major controls were clearly marked and generally easily located by every renter.  Customers lodged few complaints about the cars, with the exception of them sitting very low and the drivers seat not adjusting well to those who were short of stature.

Having logged many miles in these cars myself I found them to be great basic transportation.  The ride was good, the six cylinder models had tremendous pep, and they reliably got you from point a to point b.<br><br>
